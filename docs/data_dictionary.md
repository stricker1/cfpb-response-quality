| Field Name | Meaning | Type | Role in Project | Quality Concerns |
| ----------- | ----------- | ----------- | ----------- | ----------- |
| `Timely response?` | Whether the company gave a timely response, defined as within 15 days. (yes/no) | plain text | One of the biggest factors in determining the quality of the response given by the company. | We are not given the actual date or time the company responded, only a binary yes or no. |
| `Company response to consumer` | How the company responded (e.g. "Closed with explanation."). | plain text | Knowing the action of the response to the consumer is critial to knowing what the company did to the complaint. | We do not know if response was a positive or negative one. "Closed with explanation" could mean a whole range of things, but we're only given that it was closed with an explanation, which may or may not be satisfactory. |
| `Product` | The type of product/service the consumer identified in the complaint | plain text | To identify what the complaint itself is and to provide context to the quality of the company response. | May not be completely representative of the exact product. Sub-products exist, but not all products have sub-products. |
| `Issue` | The issue the consumer identified in the complaint. Values will vary based on the product. | plain text | To identify what the complaint itself is and to provide context to the quality of the company response. | May not be completely representative of the exact issue. Sub-issues exist, but not all issues have sub-issues. |
| `Company` | The complaint is about this company. | plain text | Identifier of the company is important in judging company response quality. | Having too many companies can potentially over-saturate data and insights. |
| `Date received` | The date the CFPB recieved the complaint | date & time in ISO 8601 format | Time-based splits is a modeling plan for the analysis conducted throughout this project. | Could response quality have changed over time? |
